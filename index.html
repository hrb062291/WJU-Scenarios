→ Board-approved 3-year plan with milestones required

CFR 3.3 — Financial Management
Expenses growing 44% vs revenue 10%. Cost structure not aligned with revenue capacity.
→ Independent financial audit; CFO-led expense benchmarking
!

This transformation plan directly addresses the requirement for strategic planning linked to financial projections.
→ Document board oversight records and planning process

3-Year Business Model Narrative (FY26–FY28)
Year 1 — FY26: Stabilize
• Board approves financial transformation plan with board resolution
• Implement admin reorganization + AI tools (target $1.6M–$2M savings)
• Stop admissions to negative-margin programs (Kinesiology/Leadership online)
• If athletics exit: announce, begin PacWest conference notice period
• WSCUC: Submit interim financial report demonstrating plan
Target: Reduce deficit to ($5M–$6M)
Year 2 — FY27: Transform
• Full admin restructure savings realized
• Targeted enrollment growth (CS, Business, Psych, online grad) begins
• Teach-out programs at reduced cost; no new admissions
• Athletics: final competitive year (if exiting) or restructured portfolio
• New program launches: AI/Data Science, Cybersecurity, RN-BSN
Target: Near break-even or ($1M–$2M)
Year 3 — FY28: Growth
• Return to operating surplus
• Full savings from FY26 decisions now fully reflected
• WSCUC reaffirmation: demonstrate sustained improvement trajectory
• Begin building financial reserves (target 60+ days cash)
• Alumni giving program expansion on improved enrollment outcomes
Target: $1M–$3M surplus
